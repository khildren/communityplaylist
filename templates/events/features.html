v0.6 — Community layer & player Latest
🌹

PDX Shelter & Resource Hub New

The 🌹 button in the header opens a live shelter and resource guide — warming centers, cooling centers, emergency shelters, hotlines, and mutual aid. Filterable by category, sortable by distance. Includes a map view with pins for every location. On extreme heat, cold, or smoke days the weather bubble pulses orange as a community alert.

🎴

Event rotator strip New

A compact event card carousel sits above the music player — rotating through tonight's events every 10 seconds. Tap ‹ › to browse manually. Tap the card to expand details inline without leaving the page. Artist names flash when their track plays.

↕

Collapsible player New

Drag the pill handle at the top of the player bar to collapse it to a slim track-name strip — reclaims screen space without stopping playback. Swipe or tap again to expand. State persists across pages. The resource hub auto-collapses the player when opened and restores it when closed.

☰

Mobile icon drawer New

On small screens, nav icons collapse behind a + toggle button. Tap to slide open a tidy drawer with all tools — shelter hub, map, submit, Discord, bug reports, and more — no cramped scroll strip.

🚗

Autopilot + rotator sync New

The map autopilot and event rotator strip are now in sync — when autopilot drives to a venue, the rotator card snaps to that same event. Green dotted GPS routes are preserved throughout.

🌫

Air quality alerts (PM2.5) New

Weather now includes real-time PM2.5 air quality from Open-Meteo. During smoke events the weather bubble pulses orange and the shelter hub flags smoke-safe cooling centers automatically.

📊

Daily visitor stats in ticker New

The scrolling banner now shows unique visitor count and average daily visits — a live pulse on how many Portlanders are using the calendar. Ticker speed scales with content length so every page scrolls at a consistent pace.

Interactive map

Leave the page idle and the map drives itself — animating real routes between venues, zooming into each one, opening an info card, and showing the weather forecast for each stop. Touch anything to take back control.
